Style Acupuncture & Herbal Clinic Website

About

Style is run by a mother-daughter duo that specializes in women’s health, acute health, and weight-loss in lower mainland Vancouver. I was approached to design an “online awning” to connect with prospective clients and to share their expertise on a digital platform, i.e. blog.

AnnaWorks on Style

My experience in the healthcare field (>3 years exposure in an orthopaedic clinic) gave me a strong jumping board in terms of design and mission statements. I conducted industry research and catalogued through websites in the wellness field. I developed a colour palette on Photoshop and compiled visual element designs to consult with Style to get an idea of the direction the duo wants to go with for their personal website. For their logo, I developed a catalogue of typographic “prototypes” on Photoshop.

From here on out

Ruby on Rails will be powering their bilingual English/Chinese website and blog.

Just Developing Stuff Landing Page

About

The landing page was designed to be just like the JDS team – fun and dynamic. JDS pursues tech work of all sorts, and their website was built to showcase the variety of work they do, everything from data analytics to filmography.

AnnaWorks on JDS

The Twitter Bootstrap framework was used to power the responsive section-dividers that display their different project types. The animations are original artwork inspired by their video review and designed on Illustrator. Within their website is a nested landing page that I built that displayed live soccer tweet analytics for the 2016 Euro Cup Quarter-, Semi-, and Finals.

Down the road

D3.js will be used to build interactive mapping of the analytics and jQuery for annotation of the line graph which will improve readability and interpretation.

Fraser Health Hackathon 2017

About

In the two-day event, my team and I planned, built, and presented our app idea and won the TELUS prize package. Our app is aimed at targeting the opioid overdose crisis that is plaguing the lower mainland in Vancouver. This was only the beginning as we are now working full steam to produce a final product.

AnnaWorks on overdose epidemic

No matter the situation, elegant and thoughtful responsive design will always be needed to add the humanistic touch to technology: instead of using common Bootstrap design, I experimented with W3.CSS for the front-end design of our hackathon entry. One of the highlights in competing at a Health Hackathon was the vast areas of interest and expertise of the contestants. You get to discover and use new tools. Within 24 hours, I got to learn and implement the open-source tool Mapbox.

Future Directions

Today, we are working with original CSS styling and jQuery to power our app’s responsive navigations.

Etsy-Shop Soloprenuer Website

About

The LovenCreativity Etsy Shop sells cards and accessories of an endless collection of cute, cheesy, adorable puns. Annie approached me with an existing prepaid Wordpress account and we discussed simple ways to create an exclusive look for her shop’s website to attract new traffic to her Etsy shop.

Photography

I did a photoshoot at her craft-fair stalls and used some of those photos for her Wordpress website in order build a more unique look. We were restricted to using free-versions of Wordpress child-themes. Restricted to no CSS or php manipulations, I added customized galleries and sidebars to include Etsy customer testimonials, reviews, and instagram posts using HTML and inline-CSS. When you think there’s no workaround, I’ll always find a way!

What's next?

Consistent maintenance of her website and updating with new visuals/content is key to bringing that fresh feel.

Nerd Digest Web App

absolute

A group of grad students who wanted a social medial platform to share interesting links amongst themselves.

AnnaWorks on shenanigans

I built a website powered by Ruby on Rails to allow posting, sharing, commenting, and voting of links. I designed it to be minimalistic and geeky with a 4-color palette and lots of whitespace. Ruby gems ‘devise’, and ‘acts as votable’ were used along with PostgreSQL as its database. I deployed the Ruby app on Heroku servers for the group’s free-access.
